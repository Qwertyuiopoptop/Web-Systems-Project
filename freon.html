Freon Info

What is Freon?

Freon is a trademarked name for a type of chloroflorocarbon (CFC) refrigerant.
It was developed in the 1920's and was widely used in various applications, including refrigeration and air conditioning systems
since 2010, Freon has been exchanged for more environmentally friendly refrigerants like R-134a, R-410A, or hydrocarbons like R-600a


Why do I not accept these items?

According to the US Evironmental Protection Agency under section 608,
Anyone handling refrigerants for maintennce, service, or repair of appliances must be Section 608 Certified
I am not Section 608 Certified, and it could cost anywhere from $100-$500 to get certified.


How should you dispose of refrigerant-containing items?

Check with your local government

You can Check with your local Government about how to safely dispose of refrigerant-containing appliances


Deliver it to a landfill that accepts refrigerant-containing items

Some landfills may change a fee for disposal, and you may need to provide proof of residence and utility bills


Call a Certified HVAC technician

For proper disposal, it's recommended to hire a certified HVAC technician who is familiar with the procedures outlined in Section 608 of the Clean Air Act.
